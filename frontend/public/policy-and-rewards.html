⚖️ Binding Agreement · Corporations & Mining Entities

Corporate Environmental Recovery
& Compliance Agreement

By partnering with RestoreGhana, participating corporations and mining entities commit to a transparent, data-driven restoration process. This is not just a sponsorship; it is a binding commitment to national recovery.

This agreement is enforceable through RestoreGhana's automated compliance infrastructure and reported directly to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). All performance data is publicly visible on the National Recovery Dashboard in real-time.
📸
Article I

Mandatory Proof-of-Restoration

To ensure absolute transparency, all Adopt-a-Region partners are required to upload time-stamped, GPS-tagged photographic and video evidence at every project milestone. Submitted data must clearly demonstrate physical environmental improvement:

  • Water de-turbidization measurements & before/after imagery
  • Soil reclamation reports verified against satellite readings
  • Evidence cross-validated by community reports and Gibby AI analysis
🚨
Article II

The Non-Compliance Fine

RestoreGhana operates an automated "Red Flag" system. A financial penalty — the Restoration Fine — is automatically triggered and reported to the EPA under any of the following conditions:

  • No verifiable progress updates submitted for a period exceeding 3 weeks
  • On-site data fails to show measurable improvement in water or soil quality
  • Submitted corporate claims are contradicted by verified community reports or satellite data
🏛️
Article III

Public Accountability

Failure to meet compliance benchmarks results in a "Non-Compliant" status on the public National Recovery Dashboard. This status:

  • Directly impacts the entity's live Restoration Score
  • Is visible to the public, press, investors, and government bodies
  • Remains flagged until verified remediation proof is submitted and approved
NON-COMPLIANT STATUS

A corporation's Restoration Score and compliance history are public record on the National Recovery Dashboard — permanently impacting corporate reputation until resolved.
